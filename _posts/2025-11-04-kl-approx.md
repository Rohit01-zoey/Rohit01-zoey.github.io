## The why?

This post is about Monte-Carlo approximations of KL divergence heaviliy inspired by (cite) and (cite). Though while reading them I felt there was a substantial gap in the mathematical rigour each provided.

$$
\text{KL}(q \vert \vert p):= \sum_x q(x) \log \left(\frac{q(x)}{p(x)} \right)
$$

We assume that we have access to $p(x)$ and $q(x)$ but the summation is not tractable as in the case with most ML models (think RL where $p$ and $q$ both are policy models)
